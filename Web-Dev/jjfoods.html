INTRODUCING JJ FOODS

JJ Foods is one of the biggest commercial food suppliers in the UK. It's main focus is on bringing affordable, high quality ingredients to local restaurants and takeaways all across the UK. Though this was a personal project of mine, I had previously worked for JJ Foods sometime back, and dealt with their clients and heard their problems. I decided that this would be a good personal project for me to do, because I had access to hands on user input and research into the companies design issues.

Objectives

  • Easy, accessible, clean interface and UI designed for customers with little time.
  • Layout designed around exposing the customer to special offers and pricing the moment they land on the page.
  • Design a professional marketplace for the customer that can be used to purchase their
  • Organic SEO search results

ALPHA PHASE

Highlighting issues with the current website

They say your first impression is usually your last and final impression. When selling a service, the last thing you ever want to do is confuse the customer and make them wonder where to go next. With the target audience for HD Knowledge, being parents, time is usually not in ample supply. The first thing to be seen as you hit the page is a call to action, along with a heading that clearly and consisely explains what they offer. Taking a mobile-first approach, an off canvas nav bar gives you access to all information at the touch of a button.

BETA PHASE

Stylization

Since HD Knowdledge are a brand new company, they didn't have a stable, solid logo. There had been some basic designs, but they were happy with me taking the reins with the logo. They had a simple sense of identity, but one that they wanted to work and expand on, and with the lack of a formal logo, this gave us room to work on and help develop their brand. Not only that, however, the colour scheme had to be clear, contrast the information perfectly with very little difficulty.

FINAL PHASE

Finalization

Starting from mobile development, the website was slowly built upon from the smallest size. Doing this meant that we could focus on the absolute essentials when it came to a website. HD Knowledge wanted to capture the customers eye quickly, wanted ti be painless to navigate, and wanted them to have instant access to contact information and avenues. Making the website responsive for mobiles first and then larger sizes made so that we could tailor the website specifically to the target audience.
